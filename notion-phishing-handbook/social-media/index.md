## General Requirements for All Social Media Platforms

### Key Evidence Required

#### 1. Letter of Authorization (LOA)

The LOA allows PhishFort to act on behalf of the client for Trademark / Copyright / DMCA infringement cases.

**What LOA Should Include**:
- Original URLs protected by PhishFort.
- Company registration number and jurisdiction.
- Registration numbers and jurisdictions of copyrights and trademarks.
- Associated brands (if applicable).

> ðŸ’¡ **Tip**
>
> **More complete = Better success rate**
>
> The more complete the LOA document, the better chances we have to achieve a successful takedown.

#### 2. Detailed Explanation of the Infringement

Context is crucial to report the correct type of infringement. Our analysts need a good understanding of the case to evaluate it and submit a compelling report.

**What to Include**:
- Type of violation (impersonation, TM, copyright, phishing).
- How it harms your brand.
- How users might be confused.
- Any customer complaints or reports.

> âš ï¸ **Warning**
>
> Sending just a link or an image without explanation will **not** suffice.

#### 3. The Original/Legitimate Profile (If Available)

Providing the real profile being impersonated helps us prove which is legitimate.

**Benefits**:
- Shorter takedown time.
- Higher success rate.
- Clearer comparison for platform reviewers.

## Common Social Media Violations

### 1. Impersonation

**What Qualifies**:
- Using victim's logo as own logo.
- Claiming to be official support/helpdesk.
- Using name/photo/position of official employee.
- Making affiliation claims.

**What Doesn't Qualify**:
- Mentioning brand in content.
- Former employees.
- Similar business names (when brand is generic).

[Evidence Guidelines](../evidence-guidelines)

---

### 2. Trademark Violation

**What Qualifies**:
- Unauthorized use of protected marks.
- Claiming official partnership without authorization.
- Confusingly similar branding.

**What Doesn't Qualify**:
- [Fair Use](../legal/fair-use) scenarios (reviews, commentary, parody).
- Legitimate resellers/distributors.
- Compatibility claims.

---

### 3. Copyright Abuse (DMCA)

**What Qualifies**:
- Unauthorized reproduction of copyrighted material.
- Copying creative works without permission.
- Distributing copyrighted content.

**What Doesn't Qualify**:
- Fair use (commentary, education, news).
- User-generated content with transformative use.
- Licensed content.

[Trademark & Copyright](../legal/trademark-copyright)

## Best Practices for Social Media Takedowns

### âœ… Do This

1. **Provide Direct Links**
   - Link to specific posts, profiles, or comments.
   - Don't just provide screenshots without URLs.

2. **Hide, Don't Delete Comments**
   - For Facebook comments, hide them instead of deleting.
   - This preserves evidence for platform authorities.

3. **Document Everything**
   - Screenshots with timestamps.
   - URLs visible in screenshots.
   - Date/time of observation.

4. **Explain the Context**
   - Why it's harmful.
   - How users might be confused.
   - Any customer reports.

### âŒ Don't Do This

1. **Don't Submit Without Context**
   - Never just send a link.
   - Always explain the violation.

2. **Don't Delete Evidence**
   - Keep original content accessible.
   - Don't block accounts before reporting.

3. **Don't Submit Too Soon**
   - Ensure you have complete evidence.
   - Have LOA ready before submission.

4. **Don't Expect Uniform Results**
   - Each platform is different.
   - Some require more evidence than others.

## When Platforms Reject Takedowns

If a takedown is rejected, it doesn't mean the case is over. Common rejection reasons:

### 1. Insufficient Evidence
**Solution**: Provide more context, screenshots, or proof of harm.

### 2. Doesn't Violate Platform Policy
**Solution**: Reframe the violation to match platform terms, or provide additional evidence showing policy violation.

### 3. Falls Under Fair Use
**Solution**: Demonstrate how it goes beyond fair use (e.g., creates consumer confusion).

### 4. LOA Issues
**Solution**: Update LOA with proper authorizations and registrations.
