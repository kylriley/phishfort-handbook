## Types of Twitter/X Violations

### 1. Profile Impersonation

**Qualifying Example** (meets 2+ criteria):
- âœ… Display name: "CustomerSupport  BrandName".
- âœ… Bio: "Official support for BrandName products".
- Profile picture: Generic image (not your logo).

This meets 2 criteria (display name + bio) = **Actionable**

**Non-Qualifying Example** (meets only 1):
- âŒ Display name: Regular person's name.
- âœ… Bio: "I work at BrandName".
- Profile picture: Personal photo.

This meets only 1 criterion = **Not Actionable**

---

### 2. Impersonating Posts

If the profile itself doesn't meet impersonation criteria, but posts do:

**Evidence Needed**:
- Link to the specific impersonating post.
- Screenshot of the post.
- Explanation of false claims.

**Example Post**:
> "This is the official BrandName account. We're giving away prizes! DM us your details."

---

### 3. Trademark Violations

If impersonation criteria aren't met, you may report as trademark violation instead.

**Evidence Needed**:
- Trademark registration documents.
- Proof of unauthorized TM use.
- LOA matching the trademark.
- How it creates consumer confusion.

## Gathering Evidence for Twitter/X

### For Profiles

1. **Screenshot the entire profile** showing:
   - Display name (top of profile).
   - @username (for reference, though not counted).
   - Bio/description.
   - Profile picture.
   - Header image.

2. **Copy the profile URL**:
   - `https://twitter.com/username` or
   - `https://x.com/username`

### For Posts

1. **Get direct link to the post**:
   - Click the post timestamp.
   - Copy URL from address bar.

2. **Screenshot the post** showing:
   - Full post text.
   - Display name.
   - Timestamp.
   - Engagement metrics.

## Common Rejection Reasons

### Only Username Matches
**Problem**: Only the @handle is similar, but display name is different

**Solution**: Not actionable unless display name or bio also qualifies.

### Only 1 Criterion Met
**Problem**: Either display name OR bio matches, but not both, and no impersonating posts

**Solution**: Find impersonating posts to meet the 2-criterion minimum.

### LOA Issues
**Problem**: Missing or incomplete Letter of Authorization

**Solution**: Provide complete LOA with all required information.

### Profile Description Doesn't Claim Affiliation
**Problem**: Bio mentions the brand but doesn't claim to represent it

**Example of NOT qualifying**:
> "Love BrandName products! Unofficial fan account"

**Solution**: Report as trademark violation instead if applicable.

## Best Practices

### âœ… Do This

1. **Verify 2+ Criteria Before Submitting**
   - Count how many criteria are met.
   - Don't submit if less than 2.

2. **Provide Links to Impersonating Posts**
   - Direct links to specific posts.
   - Shows clear false claims.

3. **Include Legitimate Profile**
   - Helps show comparison.
   - Proves your official presence.

4. **Explain Customer Confusion**
   - Show how users are misled.
   - Include customer reports if available.

### âŒ Don't Do This

1. **Don't Submit Based on Username Alone**
   - @username doesn't count.
   - Will be rejected.

2. **Don't Submit with Only 1 Criterion**
   - Must meet minimum of 2.
   - Save time by checking first.

3. **Don't Confuse Mentions with Impersonation**
   - Mentioning your brand â‰  Impersonation.
   - Must claim to BE or represent you.

4. **Don't Skip Context**
   - Explain why it's harmful.
   - Show potential for confusion.

## Alternative Approaches

If impersonation criteria aren't met, consider:

### 1. Trademark Violation Report
If your trademark is registered and being used without authorization

**Requirements**:
- TM registration documents.
- LOA.
- Proof of unauthorized use.

### 2. Copyright Violation (DMCA)
If they're using your copyrighted images, videos, or text

**Requirements**:
- Copyright proof.
- LOA.
- Specific copyrighted material being used.

### 3. Spam/Scam Reporting
If they're running scams or phishing

**Requirements**:
- Evidence of scam.
- How users are being harmed.
- Links to scam posts.

## Escalation Process

If rejected:

1. **Review the criteria**
   - Did you really meet 2+?
   - Was evidence clear?

2. **Strengthen the case**
   - Find impersonating posts.
   - Get more screenshots.
   - Document customer confusion.

3. **Consider different violation type**
   - Try TM instead of impersonation.
   - Try copyright for specific content.
   - Try spam/scam reporting.

4. **Resubmit with improvements**
   - Address rejection reasons.
   - Provide clearer evidence.
   - Add more context.
