# YouTube Takedowns

Evidence required for YouTube cases varies depending on the type of attack you are facing.

## Evidence by Attack Type

### 1. Phishing

✅ **Evidence Required**:
- Link to the legitimate account being impersonated (if possible).
- URLs with videos showing the impersonation.
- If too many videos, provide representative examples.

### 2. Scams

✅ **Evidence Required**:
- Link to the videos performing the scams.
- Explanation of how the scam scheme works.
- How it's affecting your brand (if applicable).

### 3. DMCA (Copyright Infringement)

✅ **Evidence Required**:
- Link to the video infringing the DMCA.
- Timestamp of the infringing part (if specific part of video).
- Explanation of how the video infringes your copyrights.
- Letter of Authorization to act on behalf of the brand.

### 4. Trademark Infringement

✅ **Evidence Required**:
- Link to the video or account infringing your trademarks.
- Timestamp of the infringing part (if specific part of video).
- Trademark registration for the word being infringed.
- Letter of Authorization to act on behalf of the brand.

## Best Practices

### ✅ Do This
- Provide specific timestamps for long videos.
- Include link to legitimate channel for comparison.
- Explain the harm being caused.
- Provide trademark/copyright documentation.

### ❌ Don't Do This
- Don't report entire channels without specific violating videos.
- Don't submit without proper authorization documents.
- Don't report Fair Use content (reviews, commentary, parody).

## Response Times
- Copyright (DMCA): 24-72 hours typically.
- Trademark: 5-10 days.
- Phishing/Scams: 3-7 days.
- Complex cases: Up to 14 days.
