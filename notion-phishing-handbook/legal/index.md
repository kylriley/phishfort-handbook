# Legal Frameworks

Understanding the legal frameworks that govern takedown requests is essential for building strong cases and knowing when escalation is necessary.

This section covers the key legal concepts and processes related to brand protection and takedown enforcement.

---

## Pages in This Section

Learn about trademark and copyright protections, how they apply to takedown requests, and the documentation required to enforce intellectual property rights.

**Topics covered:**
- Trademark ownership and registration
- Copyright protections
- Letter of Authorization (LOA) requirements
- Proving ownership and infringement

→ trademark-copyright

### Fair Use Doctrine

Understand what constitutes fair use and nominative use of trademarks, helping you distinguish between legitimate content and actionable violations.

**Topics covered:**
- Fair trademark use criteria
- Nominative fair use requirements
- Protected uses (news, reviews, parody, commentary)
- What cannot be taken down under fair use

→ fair-use

### ICANN & UDRP

Learn about domain dispute resolution processes including ICANN escalations and UDRP proceedings for trademark-based domain disputes.

**Topics covered:**
- ICANN escalation process and requirements
- UDRP (Uniform Domain-Name Dispute Resolution Policy)
- When to escalate domain cases
- Timelines and costs
- Bad faith registration criteria

→ icann-udrp

## When to Use Legal Frameworks

- **Trademark/Copyright issues**: Review trademark-copyright and fair-use pages
- **Domain disputes**: Check icann-udrp for escalation options
- **Unclear violations**: Use fair-use to determine if content is protected
